## Question

id: interviews-and-context/q1
type: multiple-choice
difficulty: easy
cognitive: remember
estimatedTime: 45
points: 5
concepts:
  - ethnographic-interview

What are the three levels of human data in Margaret Mead's research methodology?

### Answers

A. What people think, what people want, and what people get.

B. What people say, what people do, and what people say they do.

C. The user's age, education level, and technological expertise level.

D. Objective observations, usability assessments, and expert opinions.

Correct: B
Explanation: לפי המתודולוגיה שביססה מרגרט מיד (וכפי שמצוין בסיכום הקורס), שלושת מעגלי הנתונים האנושיים הם: מה שאנשים אומרים (Say), מה שאנשים עושים (Do), ומה שאנשים אומרים שהם עושים (Say they do). פער החדשנות נמצא במפגש ביניהם.
Learning Objective: Distinguish between the three levels of human data according to Margaret Mead.

id: interviews-and-context/q2
type: multiple-choice
difficulty: medium
cognitive: understand
estimatedTime: 60
points: 5
concepts:
  - ethnographic-interview

How is the structure and character of an ethnographic interview defined in the context of interface design?

### Answers

A. A structured and rigid interview with a list of closed questions that are easily quantified statistically.

B. "A conversation with a purpose" taking place in the user's natural environment, in a semi-structured and open-ended manner.

C. A laboratory performance test where the interviewer asks questions only after task completion.

D. An anonymous rating-based survey sent via email to a broad audience.

Correct: B
Explanation: הראיון האתנוגרפי מוגדר כ"שיחה עם מטרה" (Conversation with a purpose). הוא מתבצע בשטח (סביבה טבעית) ומתאפיין בפתיחות וגמישות (חצי-מובנה או לא-מובנה) כדי לגלות ידע סמוי, בניגוד לראיונות מובנים (A), מעבדות (C) או סקרים (D).
Learning Objective: Explain how to conduct an effective ethnographic interview using open-ended questions.

## Question

id: interviews-and-context/q3
type: scenario
difficulty: hard
cognitive: apply
estimatedTime: 90
points: 5
concepts:
  - ethnographic-interview

An interface designer wants to research how people use a navigation app during their daily commutes to define a "road hazard reporting" feature. Which of the following questions is the best formulation for a field ethnographic interview?

### Scenario

An interface designer wants to research how people use a navigation app during their daily commutes to define a "road hazard reporting" feature.

### Answers

A. On a scale of 1 to 5, how much would you like a red report button in the corner of the screen?

B. Imagine a situation where you wanted to report a pothole. How would you expect the app to respond?

C. Describe to me your last drive to a new place. What apps did you use, what did you notice along the way, and how did you react when you encountered an unexpected obstacle on the road?

D. Do you think reporting road hazards improves driver safety by 20% or 50%?

Correct: C
Explanation: שאלה C היא שאלה פתוחה, מבוססת-נרטיב של חוויה אמיתית מהעבר הקרוב ("הנסיעה האחרונה שלך"), המעודדת את המשתמש לספר סיפור ולהציג את ההקשר האמיתי. שאלות A ו-D הן סגורות ומנחות, ושאלה B היא היפותטית (אנשים גרועים בלחזות את התנהגותם העתידית עקב פער החדשנות).
Learning Objective: Explain how to conduct an effective ethnographic interview using open-ended questions.

## Question

id: interviews-and-context/q4
type: multiple-choice
difficulty: hard
cognitive: analyze
estimatedTime: 90
points: 5
concepts:
  - participant-observation

What is the central criticism of the Participant Observation method in the context of the "objectivity vs. participation tension" (based on the Margaret Mead case study in Samoa)?

### Answers

A. Active participation in the field takes too much time and does not allow the researcher to write reports on time.

B. Over-immersion in the community ("Going Native") can impair the researcher's scientific detachment, leading to subjective confirmation bias in interpreting findings.

C. Field participation causes users to stop working and ask the researcher guidance questions.

D. Participant observation prevents the researcher from using cameras or digital recording tools.

Correct: B
Explanation: הביקורת המרכזית על מרגרט מיד בסמואה (ועל תצפית משתתפת לא מאוזנת בכלל) היא שהיטמעות עמוקה מדי בקהילה גורמת לאובדן האובייקטיביות המדעית, ולכך שהחוקר מפרש את המציאות דרך משקפי ההטיות והציפיות האישיות שלו במקום לתעד עובדות יבשות.
Learning Objective: Analyze the tension between objectivity and participation in participant observation (Margaret Mead's Samoan dilemma).

## Question

id: interviews-and-context/q5
type: scenario
difficulty: hard
cognitive: analyze
estimatedTime: 90
points: 5
concepts:
  - gatekeeper
  - ethnographic-research

Why is this situation methodologically problematic, and how should the researcher proceed?

### Scenario

A researcher arrived at a telephone customer service center to investigate usability issues in the call management system. The shift supervisor (gatekeeper) welcomed her and directed her to observe the center's top-performing agent, who has worked there for 5 years.

### Answers

A. The situation is not problematic at all; observing the outstanding representative will reveal the most efficient shortcuts of the system.

B. The situation is problematic because the outstanding representative will demand additional payment for her cooperation.

C. The situation is problematic because it creates a selection bias (Selection Bias) that presents an ideal picture; the researcher should explain to the gatekeeper that to identify issues she must observe a variety of workers, including new hires or those struggling with the system.

D. The situation is problematic because veteran employees tend to use keyboard shortcuts only, preventing the researcher from seeing the interface buttons on the screen.

Correct: C
Explanation: שומרי סף נוטים להציג את הארגון שלהם בצורה הטובה ביותר (הטיית מדגם). תצפית רק במשתמש מנוסה מאוד (Power User) תעוות את דרישות המערכת ותגרום לה להישאר בלתי שמישה עבור הרוב. על החוקר לנהל משא ומתן פעיל מול שומר הסף כדי להבטיח מדגם מגוון.
Learning Objective: Define the role of gatekeepers (Gatekeepers) and their impact on field research access and selection bias.
Misconception: Students sometimes believe that learning from super-users is the best way to design interfaces, missing the fact that systems also need to be usable for beginners (Learnability).
